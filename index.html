The Problem

Lost items on campus go unnoticed for days

Notice boards, WhatsApp broadcasts, and word-of-mouth are fragmented and unreliable. Items get lost in the shuffle, owners have no clear place to look, and finders don't know who to contact.

FindIt centralizes the entire process — from the moment an item is reported to the moment it is returned — in one transparent, accessible platform.

01
Fragmented Reporting

Lost item notices are scattered across boards, group chats, and hallway conversations with no central record.

02
No Matching System

Lost and found reports exist in isolation. Without a comparison engine, potential matches go unseen.

03
Ownership Disputes

Without a verification step, items can be claimed by the wrong person. FindIt enforces proof of ownership.

Features

Built for the full recovery cycle

Every feature is designed around one goal — getting lost items back to their owners quickly and securely.

Lost Item Reporting

Submit a structured report with item name, category, description, last known location, date, and photos. Each report receives a unique tracking ID for follow-up.

Found Item Reporting

Report an item you found on campus in seconds. It is stored securely and immediately scanned against all active lost reports to find a possible owner.

Smart Matching

The system compares item type, location, time, and description attributes to surface the most likely matches and ranks them by confidence score.

Claim Verification

Claimants must answer security questions and provide ownership details the system validates. Items are only released to verified owners. All exchanges are logged.

Real-time Notifications

Get notified the moment a potential match surfaces for your report, when someone files a related submission, or when your claim status is updated.

QR Code Recovery

Register valuables with a unique QR code sticker. A finder can scan it to instantly trigger a notification to the owner through FindIt — no app required.

How It Works

From report to recovery in four steps

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I report a lost item?

Click Report Lost Item on the homepage to open the report form. Fill in the item name, category, date, location, and any identifying details. Your report gets a unique tracking ID you can use to check its status at any time.

What happens after I submit a report?

FindIt immediately scans all existing found-item reports and compares attributes like item type, location, and date. If a potential match is found, you will receive a notification so you can verify and claim the item.

How does the smart matching system work?

The matching engine compares multiple attributes — item category, description keywords, last known location, and time window — and scores each potential match. Reports are ranked by confidence score and the top matches are surfaced first.

Is FindIt only for students?

No. FindIt is designed for the entire campus community — students, faculty, staff, and administration. Each group has a specific role: students report and claim, faculty manage found items in their department, and admin monitors campus-wide activity.

How is ownership verified before returning an item?

Claimants must answer a set of ownership questions and provide specific details about the item — brand, color, model, contents — that only the real owner would know. The system validates these against the original report before authorizing the handover.

What is the QR Code Recovery feature?

You can register your valuables on FindIt and print a unique QR sticker to attach to them. If a finder scans the QR code with any smartphone camera, they are directed to a page where they can log the item as found — automatically notifying you, without the finder needing a FindIt account.
